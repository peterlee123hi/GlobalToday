NASA's 5th Failed Attempt for Mars Colonization

by John Doe, Andrew Jackson, Brad Lee

3:14 p.m. EST November 13, 2039

In another daring attempt to establish a colony on Mars, Martian 5 launched in Houston, TX earlier this morning. However, due to an unforeseen technical malfunction, the spacecraft that held billions of dollars in equipment exploded after 6 minutes of flight time.

Since the drastic climate change, the global aerospace community has been focused on settling a colony on Mars for safety measures. A large portion of humanity's resources have been devoted to this project that may be our last hope for survival. However, with the recent failures in multiple stages of the process, the hope for mankind to colonize Mars is diminishing.

The scientific community is now reconsidering the general consensus on the approach to solving the climate change crisis. Especially considering recent events, the task to terraform and colonize Mars still seems out of reach. Some argue that more resources should have been devoted to dismantle the presence of fossil fuel in our society and establish an eco-friendly community.

Geoengineering Plans Rejected by Congress

by John Doe, Andrew Jackson, Brad Lee

3:14 p.m. EST November 13, 2039

This past weekend, the National Science Foundation proposed a government sponsored attempt to lower the global temperature through solar radiation management. Due to international complications and the high risk involved, congress voted against proceeding with the execution of the project.

In the past few decades, scientists agree that solar radiation management is a feasible solution to climate change. This would involve injecting particles into the atmosphere that reflect a fraction of the sun's rays. In effect, the global temperature would decrease by a few degrees. This technique was inspired by a historical volcanic eruption in the Philippines when Mount Pinatubo sent high volumes of sulfure dioxide into the atmosphere and lowered the global temperature by around 2°F.

Global Temperatures Rise to 91°F

by John Doe, Andrew Jackson, Brad Lee

3:14 p.m. EST November 13, 2039

New research conducted by NASA shows that the average temperature across the Earth is now 91°F, the highest recorded global temperature in history. Evacuations are still in effect in cities close the maritime boundary such as San Francisco and New York as the sea level continues to rise rapidly.

Since the global temperature rised above 85°F, ecosystems across the world have experienced major destabilization and many species become extinct every year. As a result, food production is centralized in sustainable methods and closed environments. The latest advancements in genetically modified food that increases yield also allow food production levels to meet the needs of society.

The rapid increase in animal wildlife conservatories over the past few years is diminishing the loss of biodiversity. Scientists are continuing to closely monitor wildlife in remote areas and develop the means to protect the few remaining species.

Fossil Fuel Industry Struggles to Find Resources

by John Doe, Andrew Jackson, Brad Lee

3:14 p.m. EST November 13, 2039

The fossil fuel industry may finally lose its dominance in the global economy as the last remote areas that contain fossil fuel reach depletion. Scientists agree that, since the Industrial Revolution, society will be forced to convert to become 100% eco-friendly by using sustainable means of energy.

However, prominent engineers question whether the level of technology in society is advanced enough to provide renewable energy globally. Many communities that have relied on fossil fuels for decades are not fully prepared to convert to renewable energy. As a result, this may cause the populations in major cities to increase further and leave many communities without energy.

President Donald Trump announced that the government will relocate the nation's resources from Mars colonization to renewable energy development.
